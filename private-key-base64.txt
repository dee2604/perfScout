brew install gnupg

### 2. Generate a New GPG Key
Run this command in your terminal:
```sh
gpg --full-generate-key
```
You’ll be prompted for:
- **Key type**: Press Enter for the default (RSA and RSA).
- **Key size**: 4096 is recommended (type 4096 and press Enter).
- **Expiration**: Choose how long the key should be valid (or press Enter for no expiration).
- **Name**: Enter your name.
- **Email**: Enter your email address.
- **Comment**: (Optional) Press Enter to skip.
- **Passphrase**: Choose a secure passphrase.

### 3. List Your Keys
After generation, list your keys to get the key ID:
```sh
gpg --list-secret-keys --keyid-format=long
```
You’ll see output like:
```
/Users/yourname/.gnupg/secring.gpg
----------------------------------
sec   rsa4096/ABCDEF1234567890 2024-06-07 [SC]
      Your Name <your@email.com>
```
The part after `rsa4096/` (e.g., `ABCDEF1234567890`) is your key ID.

### 4. Export Your Private Key
To export your private key to `private-key.asc`:
```sh
gpg --armor --export-secret-keys YOUR_KEY_ID > private-key.asc
```
Replace `YOUR_KEY_ID` with your actual key ID from the previous step.
